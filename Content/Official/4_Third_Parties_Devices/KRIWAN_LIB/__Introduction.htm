Introductory guide

Introduction

HVAC/R compressors need to be protected. This is typically done with a compressor protection relay like the KRIWAN™ INT69. If the compressor protection relay trips, it is first necessary that the critical situation ends: e.g. the motor cools down. The compressor is stopped until this happens. After this there are two options: the compressor can be stopped only temporarily and after a certain time, e.g. 5 min, the compressor is activated again and can be restarted. The other option is that the compressor is locked permanently and a manual reset is necessary.

For advanced maintenance of the compressor, additionally, it is possible to use these and many more data provided by

  • the INT69 Diagnose: failure list, statistics about running time or switching frequency and oil management in a compressor
  • the INT280 Oil level regulator: oil status, failure list, statistics about running time or switching frequency of oil valve for oil management of compressor motor

and many more help to maintain and even prevent issues with the compressor.

In combination with the KRIWAN™ Modbus Gateway INT600DM, and a controller, the reset of a compressor can be more advanced.

 

KRIWAN_LIB is a c.suite library that includes function blocks for Modbus communication between a c.pCO family controller and KRIWAN™ devices, using a KRIWAN™ gateway.

 

The KRIWAN™ supported devices are:
