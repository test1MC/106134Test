General operations

EnvAlrm builds the envelope vertex points from the data provided in the CfgEnv structure (see description): the implemented logic accepts dynamic limits, within the following limits:

  • Maximum condensing temperature (zone 3);
  • Maximum evaporation temperature (zone 5);
  • Minimum evaporation temperature (zone 9);

The controlled zones are described in the following table, with reference to the illustration above:

Zone Description
1 Zone within operating limits
2 High compression ratio
3 High discharge pressure
3 Eco High discharge pressure with Eco on
4 High motor current
5 High suction pressure
5 Eco High suction pressure with Eco on
6 Low compression ratio
7 Low differential pressure
8 Low discharge pressure
9 Low suction pressure

The EconRun pin informs the control logic that the economiser circuit is turned on, thus requiring envelope to adjust to the new limits specific to Eco operations (see the diagram).

The envelope vertex coordinates calculated and used by the function block are written in the EnvP_X and EnvP_Y function block outputs from position 0 to 7 as x and y values corresponding to the points indicated in the envelope examples shown in the following illustrations. The envelope on the right shows how values are assigned to the coinciding points due to lack of the relevant segment.
