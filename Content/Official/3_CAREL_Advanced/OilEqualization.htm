General operations

OilEqualization is designed to recover the excess oil (through the overflow pipe) from the compressor crankcase at high pressure, typically in BLDC Tandem/Trio configuration.

It operates a solenoid valve (in a special way) when several compressors are running. The solenoid valve takes the oil from the overflow pipe of each compressor crankcase and brings it back into circulation (i.e. in the common rail on the suction side).

The figure shows the oil path in orange .

 

When the circuit is operating (BLDC is working), on switching on the 2nd or 3rd OnOff compressor, the valve is energised for the start-up time (StartUpT_OLEQ). Then cyclically for a certain time (OnT_OLEQ), with a pause time that increases over time, based on the time needed to reach the working condition (WarmUpT_OLEQ), from a minimum value (MinOffT_OLEQ) to a maximum value (MaxOffT_OLEQ).

Examples

 

Example 1: BLDC Tandem/Trio circuit

The FB receives some information in input from the status of the OnOff compressors in the circuit:

  • Comp2Onfrom status of the second compressor in the circuit (first OnOff)
  • Comp3Onfrom status of the third compressor in the circuit (second OnOff)

And returns the status of the solenoid valve that manage oil equalisation:

  • OilEquVlv_OLEQ to command the digital output for the solenoid
