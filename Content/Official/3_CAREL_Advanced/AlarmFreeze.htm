General operations

AlarmFreeze manages the evaporator antifreeze protection with the compressor running, checks the saturated evaporation temperature, suitably filtered to consider evaporator thermal inertia, and generates the antifreeze alarm when there are conditions in which there is the risk of ice formation.

 

The evaporation pressure probe runs the antifreeze control because it directly reads evaporator conditions. The water delivery probe is not considered because it does not accurately measure the potential or actual formation of ice on the evaporator.

When evaporation temperature in the circuit reaches antifreeze conditions, an alarm is triggered and the circuit is stopped.

The evaporation temperature is filtered, according to the exponential distribution formula, to consider the evaporator thermal mass and avoid sudden alarms at start-up. A specific algorithm uses this filtered value and triggers when the antifreeze threshold is exceeded. Following is a diagram of how the evaporation temperature filter works.

 

When the control temperature drops under the set (Thrsh_ALFZ), a counter is triggered and its time-out varies following a hyperbolic rule according to the evaporation temperature's distance from the antifreeze threshold. At the maximum delta (Diff_ALFZ), the counter time-out is zero.

 

The hyperbolic rule imitates real freezing behavior and provides better protection. The following diagram illustrates the delay trend according to the distance from alarm threshold with a delay T_1K_ALFZ=60s and differential Diff_ALFZ=30K. When the saturated evaporation temperature is equal to the antifreeze threshold (Thrsh_ALFZ), the delay is more or less 10 times the set value (about 600s in the example).

 

The value suggested in the example refers to a pipe evaporator; if a plate evaporator is used, with a smaller thermal mass, the time (T_1K_ALFZ) must be reduced to an adequate value.

The following table provides the values suggested for the delay and differential according to the type of evaporator used.

For pure water, the antifreeze threshold should be set just under zero (from -0.3°C to -0.8°C) to consider the thermal gradient of heat transmission through metal between the coolant and water. For plate exchangers, values should be set closer to zero (over -0.3°C) to ensure greater protection due to the specific mechanical construction.

 

A correct antifreeze threshold needs to take into consideration the minimum temperature reached inside the evaporator: while with refrigerants without glide or with minimal glide (e.g. R410A, R134a) this value coincides with the pressure-temperature conversion performed by the transducer installed on the suction line, for refrigerants with glide (e.g. R407C), the value is lower than the conversion (in the case of R407C by 5-6°C).

As a consequence, the suggested antifreeze set point with pure water and R407C refrigerant is 4-4.5°C.

Use examples

 

Example 1: Chiller/HeatPump unit

The FB receives in input the probe value (EvapTemp_ALFZ). It starts to run when the circuit goes On and the unit type enables one or other of the FBs (CH or HP FB). Based of the condition it returns the alarm (Alrm_ALFZ) that switches Off the compressor/s in the circuit.
