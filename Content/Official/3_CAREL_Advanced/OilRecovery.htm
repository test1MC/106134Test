OilRecovery

Recovers the oil that remains in the pipes/heat exchangers during low load working periods.

General operations

OilRecovey ensures correct oil circulation in the pipes of the refrigerant circuit, for circuits with a modulating compressor, in order to make sure the oil returns to the compressor crankcase.

 

In the refrigerant circuit, to keep the oil flow in the pipes and bring it back to the compressor, a minimum refrigerant speed is required, otherwise if the speed of the refrigerant drops below the design value oil flow stops, and this causes:

  • Lack of compressor lubrication;
  • Oil in the condenser and evaporator that worsens heat exchange thus decreasing unit efficiency.

 

When the unit request (PwrReq_OLEQ) and the actual speed of the compressor (CompSpeed_OLEQ) remain below the thresholds (MinReqThrsh_OLEQ, CompMinSpeedThrsh_OLEQ) for a period of time (WaitT_OLEQ), the function forces the compressor to a fixed speed (FrcCompSpeed_OLEQ) for another period of time (FrcSpeedT_OLEQ) to recover the oil lost along the refrigerant circuit.

It is also possible to manually activate this procedure (FrcOilRecMan_OLEQ).

The FB considers the compressor Off when speed is below 1.0 rps.

 

The diagram represents a circuit with only one compressor. For this reason the PwrReq_OLRC and ReqIn_OLRC have the same value, but in the case of multiple compressors, the two variables will have different values.

Examples

 

Example 1: circuit with a BLDC compressor

The FB receives some information in input from the control and BLDC:

  • ReqIn_OLRC from control request for BLDC
  • PwrReq_OLRC from control request
  • CompSpeed_OLRCfrom Info_PWRP.Info_RotorSpeed_rps
  • _OLRCCompMaxSpeedfrom Info_PWRP.UI_MotMaxOutFreq_rps

And returns the request to the BLDC:

  • ReqOut_OLEQ to PowerRequest_BLDC
